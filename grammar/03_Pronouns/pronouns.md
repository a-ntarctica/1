# Pronouns

## Personal pronouns

| Nominative | Object | Translation |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| I | me | я / мне |
| you | you | ты / тебе |
| he | him | он / ему |
| she | her | она / ей |
| it | it | оно |
| we | us | мы / нам |
| they | them | они / им |

## Possessive pronouns

| Basic form | Absolute form |
|-----------|--------------|
| my | mine |
| your | yours |
| his | his |
| her | hers |
| its | its |
| our | ours |
| their | theirs |

## Some vs Any

| Pronoun | When | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| some | affirmative sentences | I have got some new pictures |
| some | polite requests | Can I have some more tea? |
| any | questions (real doubt) | Have you got any new dresses? |
| any | affirmative (= any at all) | Take any cake you like |

## Much/Little vs Many/Few

| Pronoun | With | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| much / little / a little | uncountable | I have little time |
| many / few / a few | countable | She told me many interesting facts |

## Demonstrative pronouns

| Pronoun | Usage | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| this/these | near, present | This is our house |
| that/those | far, past | That is our school |
| such | quality | Such a clever dog |
| the same | identity | The same phrase |

## Reciprocal & Negative

| Pronoun | Usage |
|---------|-------|
| each other | 2 people — Kate and Nick saw each other every evening |
| one another | 3+ people — The five puppies liked one another |
| nobody / no one | с одушевленными — There was nobody in the room |
| nothing | с неодушевленными — I know nothing about it |
