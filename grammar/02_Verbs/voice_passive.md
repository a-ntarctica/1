# Active and Passive Voice

## Core idea
- **Active:** the subject DOES the action — *Pete has broken the window*
- **Passive:** the subject RECEIVES the action — *The window has been broken by Pete*

## Passive structure: `to be` (any tense) + past participle (III form)

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | I ask | I am asked |
| Past Simple | I asked | I was asked |
| Future Simple | I shall ask | I shall be asked |
| Present Continuous | I am asking | I am being asked |
| Past Continuous | I was asking | I was being asked |
| Present Perfect | I have asked | I have been asked |
| Past Perfect | I had asked | I had been asked |
| Future Perfect | I will have asked | I will have been asked |

> Note: Perfect Continuous passive is not commonly used.

## English vs Russian passive — key differences

| English | Russian equivalent |
|---------|--------------------|
| He was given a book (Indirect Passive) | — (no equivalent) |
| The doctor was sent for (Prepositional Passive) | — (no equivalent) |
| The book was given to him (Direct Passive) | Книга была дана ему |
