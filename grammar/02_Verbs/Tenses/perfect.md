# Perfect Tenses

## Quick reference

| Tense | Usage | Signal words | Example |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Present Perfect | past action connected to NOW | just, already, yet, ever, never, since, for | I have just bought this book |
| Past Perfect | action completed BEFORE another past moment | by the time, already, after | She had made coffee when I arrived |
| Future Perfect | action completed BEFORE a future moment | by tomorrow, by 5 o'clock | She will have gone home by the time I arrive |
| Future Perfect in the Past | reported speech | — | She said she would have done this by Friday |

## Structure: `have/has/had` + past participle (III form)

### Present Perfect

| Subject | Affirmative | Question | Negative |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| I / You / We / They | have written | Have I written? | I have not written |
| He / She / It | has written | Has he written? | He has not written |

### Past Perfect

| Subject | Affirmative | Question | Negative |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| All | had written | Had I written? | I had not written |

### Future Perfect

| Subject | Affirmative | Question | Negative |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| I / We | shall/will have written | Shall I have written? | I shall not have written |
| Others | will have written | Will you have written? | You will not have written |

## Present Perfect vs Past Simple

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Connection to NOW | Completed, no connection to now |
| No specific time | Specific time mentioned |
| I have seen that film | I saw that film yesterday |
