# Adverbs

## Classification

| Type | Question | Examples |
|------|----------|---------|
| Manner | How? | well, quickly, slowly, fast, badly, deeply, sincerely |
| Frequency | How often? | often, never, always, sometimes, ever, constantly, twice |
| Time | When? How long? | today, yesterday, soon, now, late, tomorrow, already, yet, suddenly |
| Place | Where? | here, there, downstairs, outside, far, back, everywhere, abroad |
| Degree | How much? | little, much, hardly, very, too, just, almost, quite, rather, enough, extremely |

## Formation

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Simple (root only) | here, there, out, quite, soon, little |
| Derived (root + suffix/prefix) | slowly, upstairs, badly, usually, daily |
| Compound (two roots) | sometimes, anywhere, meantime, at least, at first |

## Key adverbs — position rules
- Frequency adverbs go BEFORE main verb, AFTER auxiliary: She **always** comes late. She has **always** been late.
- Degree adverbs go BEFORE the adjective/adverb they modify: **very** good, **quite** slowly
